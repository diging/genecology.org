Our Research Methods

We use a wide range of materials and methods to investigate the history of ecological genetics. In addition to published scientific papers, scientists sometimes leave behind letters, notes, and photographs--preserved in archives--that help us to piece together what they were doing and thinking at certain moments in their careers. We also collect oral histories from both retired and practicing scientists who were involved in ecological genetics research. Making sense of all of these materials is hard work, and we do that in two ways: (1) through careful and systematic reading, and (2) using digital tools and methods to collect and analyze information about scientific activity. Each of these approaches has its own advantages and drawbacks, but by using them together we can gain an even richer understand of the history of this scientific field.

Throughout the project we will update this page with details about the tools and techniques that we use to reconstruct the history of ecological genetics.
